Grade 3 Phonics Review

Organized for easy explanation

1. Vowel Digraphs (Two Vowels, One Sound)
Pattern Words Key Concept
Long E (ee, ea, ie) peaches agree freezing "When two vowels go walking, the first one does the talking."
Long O (oa, ow) shadow reason pillow roaring board soared These combinations make the long 'O' sound.
Long A (ai, ay) braided display These make the long 'A' sound. The 'ay' is often at the end of a word.
2. R-Controlled Vowels (The Bossy R)
Pattern Words Key Concept
ar target alarm darkness forget trailer The 'R' makes the 'A' sound like the word *car*.
ore / or before adore explore Morning format absorb The 'R' controls the vowel, making the 'O' sound like *door* or *fork*.
3. Syllable Patterns (Where to Divide a Word)
Pattern Words Explanation
VCV (Vowel-Consonant-Vowel) ti/ger li/zard fi/nal fo/cus **Split BEFORE the consonant.** This often leaves the first vowel **LONG** (says its name).
VCCV (Vowel-Consonant-Consonant-Vowel) fin/ish yel/low but/ton **Split BETWEEN the two consonants.** This often leaves the first vowel **SHORT** (makes its sound).
Tip: Explain the "Why" behind the split to make the rules stick!
